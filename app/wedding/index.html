Getting there

Driving yourself

There’s incredibly limited street meter parking outside the theater and throughout the neighborhood, but it’s hard to come by, so plan to circle for a while if that’s your thing. There are a very few paid lots nearby, but please call them ahead as restrictions on time and payment can be confusing.

Being driven

If you're staying in town, a cab may be a better bet. You can get all the way across town for about $20. Cabs CANNOT be hailed in Milwaukee. Call American United at (414) 220-5000 or Milwaukee Cab Co-op at (414) 271-1800 with your pick up time, location and payment method. -or-

Good news! Uber just launched in Milwaukee. If you haven’t signed up yet, you can use Aki’s affiliate link to get a $20 credit on your account. Uber allows you to get a car or taxi right from your (iOS or Android) smartphone. Quite handy.

Hopping a bus

Routes 21, 30, and the Green Line will all get you within a block of the front door.
